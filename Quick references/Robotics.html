Robotics Kinematics

ConceptDescriptionFormulaApplications

Inverse KinematicsCalculate joint angles from end-effector positionθ = f⁻¹(x, y, z)Path planning, manipulation tasks
Denavit-Hartenberg ParametersMethod to define robot link frames4 parameters per joint (α, a, d, θ)Robot modeling, simulation
WorkspaceVolume of space robot can reachReachable vs dexterous workspaceTask planning, robot selection
RedundancyMore degrees of freedom than requiredDOF > task requirementsObstacle avoidance, optimization

Robot Control Systems

Control TypeDescriptionCharacteristicsApplications
Open-Loop ControlControl without feedbackNo error correction, simplePre-programmed tasks, simple movements
Closed-Loop ControlControl with feedbackError correction, more accuratePosition control, trajectory following
Proportional Control (P)Control proportional to errorFast response, may have steady-state errorSimple position control
Proportional-Integral (PI)Proportional + integral actionEliminates steady-state errorVelocity control, force control
Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID)Complete feedback controlOptimal response, widely usedMotor control, trajectory tracking
Adaptive ControlAdjusts parameters in real-timeHandles changing dynamicsUncertain environments, changing loads
Optimal ControlMinimizes cost functionMathematically optimal solutionEnergy efficiency, time optimization
