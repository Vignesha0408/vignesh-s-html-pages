Wireless Networks - Quick Reference

ConceptDescription
Definition
Network that uses radio waves, microwaves, or infrared signals to connect devices without physical cables. Enables mobility and flexibility.
Characteristics
Shared medium, limited bandwidth, interference, mobility, security challenges
Frequency Bands
ISM bands (2.4GHz, 5GHz), cellular bands (700MHz-3.5GHz), satellite bands
Wireless TechnologiesDescription
WiFi (802.11)
Local wireless networking using various standards (a/b/g/n/ac/ax/ay)
Cellular (1G-5G)
Mobile communication networks with increasing data rates and capabilities
Bluetooth
Short-range wireless communication for device pairing
Zigbee
Low-power, low-data-rate wireless networking for IoT devices
WiFi StandardsDescription
802.11a
5GHz, up to 54 Mbps, OFDM modulation
802.11b
2.4GHz, up to 11 Mbps, DSSS modulation
802.11g
2.4GHz, up to 54 Mbps, OFDM modulation
802.11n
2.4GHz/5GHz, up to 600 Mbps, MIMO technology
802.11ac
5GHz, up to 6.9 Gbps, MU-MIMO
802.11ax
WiFi 6, 2.4GHz/5GHz, up to 9.6 Gbps, OFDMA
Cellular GenerationsDescription
1G
Analog voice communication (1980s)
2G
Digital voice and SMS (GSM, CDMA)
3G
Mobile internet, up to 2 Mbps
4G/LTE
IP-based, up to 100 Mbps mobile, 1 Gbps stationary
5G
Ultra-low latency, massive IoT, up to 20 Gbps
Network TopologiesDescription
Infrastructure
Devices connect through access points (APs) to wired network
Ad-hoc
Peer-to-peer connections without central infrastructure
Mesh
Devices relay data for other devices, self-healing network
Cellular
Base stations cover geographic areas (cells)
ChallengesDescription
Interference
Signal degradation from other devices, physical obstacles
Security
Vulnerabilities to eavesdropping, unauthorized access
Mobility
Handoff between access points/base stations
Power Management
Battery conservation in mobile devices
Protocols & StandardsDescription
MAC Protocols
CSMA/CA (WiFi), TDMA (cellular), frequency hopping
Security
WEP, WPA, WPA2, WPA3 for WiFi; AKA for cellular
Quality of Service
802.11e for WiFi, QoS classes in cellular
Emerging TechnologiesDescription
WiFi 6E/7
6GHz band, higher throughput, lower latency
5G Advanced
Enhanced 5G with improved capabilities
Li-Fi
Light-based wireless communication
Satellite Internet
Low Earth Orbit (LEO) constellations (Starlink)
