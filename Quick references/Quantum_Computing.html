Quantum Computing - Quick Reference

ConceptDescription
Definition
Computing technology based on quantum theory principles. Uses quantum bits (qubits) that can exist in multiple states simultaneously.
Key Principles
Superposition, Entanglement, Interference, Quantum tunneling
Applications
Cryptography, optimization, drug discovery, machine learning, materials science
Quantum StatesDescription
Qubit
Quantum bit that can be 0, 1, or both simultaneously (superposition)
Superposition
Qubit exists in multiple states until measured
Entanglement
Qubits become correlated, measurement of one affects the other instantly
Quantum Tunneling
Qubits can pass through energy barriers
Quantum GatesDescription
Pauli Gates
X, Y, Z gates that perform bit flips and phase flips
Hadamard Gate
Creates superposition (H gate)
CNOT Gate
Controlled NOT gate for entanglement (2-qubit gate)
Phase Gates
Introduce phase shifts (S, T gates)
Quantum AlgorithmsDescription
Shor's Algorithm
For integer factorization, threatens RSA encryption
Grover's Algorithm
For searching unsorted databases with quadratic speedup
Quantum Fourier Transform
Foundation for many quantum algorithms
Variational Quantum Eigensolver
For quantum chemistry and optimization problems
Quantum HardwareDescription
Superconducting
IBM, Google use superconducting circuits cooled to near absolute zero
Trapped Ions
IonQ uses trapped ions manipulated with lasers
Photonic
Uses photons as qubits, operated at room temperature
Topological
Theoretical approach using anyons, more stable qubits
ChallengesDescription
Decoherence
Loss of quantum state due to environmental interference
Error Correction
Quantum error correction requires many physical qubits per logical qubit
Scalability
Building large-scale quantum computers remains challenging
Temperature
Most systems require extremely low temperatures to operate
Quantum SupremacyDescription
Definition
Point where quantum computers outperform classical computers for specific tasks
Google Sycamore
Claimed quantum supremacy in 2019 with random circuit sampling
NISQ Era
Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum devices with 50-100 qubits
Future ApplicationsDescription
Cryptography
Quantum key distribution, post-quantum cryptography
Drug Discovery
Simulating molecular interactions for pharmaceuticals
Financial Modeling
Portfolio optimization, risk analysis
AI/ML
Quantum machine learning algorithms
