Computational Biology - Quick Reference

ConceptDescription
Definition
Interdisciplinary field combining biology, computer science, and mathematics to analyze biological data and model biological systems.
Purpose
Analyze biological data, predict protein structures, understand genetic variations, model biological processes
Applications
Drug discovery, personalized medicine, evolutionary biology, genomics, proteomics
BioinformaticsDescription
Sequence Analysis
Analysis of DNA, RNA, and protein sequences using algorithms and databases
Genome Assembly
Reconstructing complete genomes from short sequence reads
Phylogenetics
Study of evolutionary relationships between organisms using molecular data
Database Resources
NCBI, EMBL-EBI, DDBJ, UniProt for storing and accessing biological data
GenomicsDescription
Sequencing Technologies
Sanger sequencing, Next-generation sequencing (NGS), Third-generation sequencing
Variant Calling
Identifying genetic variations (SNPs, indels, structural variants) in DNA sequences
Gene Expression
Analysis of RNA-seq data to measure gene expression levels
Comparative Genomics
Comparing genomes across species to identify similarities and differences
ProteomicsDescription
Protein Structure
Prediction of 3D protein structures from amino acid sequences
Protein Folding
Understanding how proteins achieve their functional 3D structure
Protein-Protein Interactions
Analysis of how proteins interact and form complexes
Protein Function Prediction
Predicting protein function from sequence and structure
Structural BiologyDescription
Molecular Modeling
Computational modeling of molecular structures and interactions
Docking Studies
Predicting how molecules (ligands) bind to target proteins
Molecular Dynamics
Simulating molecular motion and interactions over time
Drug Design
Rational drug design using computational methods
Systems BiologyDescription
Network Analysis
Modeling biological networks (metabolic, regulatory, protein-protein)
Pathway Modeling
Mathematical modeling of biological pathways and processes
Metabolic Modeling
Constraint-based modeling of metabolic networks
Multi-omics Integration
Combining different types of biological data (genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics)
Algorithms & ToolsDescription
Sequence Alignment
BLAST, Smith-Waterman, Needleman-Wunsch for comparing sequences
Multiple Sequence Alignment
Clustal, MUSCLE, MAFFT for aligning multiple sequences
Phylogenetic Analysis
MEGA, PhyML, BEAST for building evolutionary trees
Structure Prediction
AlphaFold, Rosetta, I-TASSER for protein structure prediction
Emerging AreasDescription
Metagenomics
Analysis of genetic material from environmental samples
Single-Cell Analysis
Analysis of biological data at single-cell resolution
AI in Biology
Machine learning applications in drug discovery and genomics
Precision Medicine
Personalized treatment based on individual genetic profiles
