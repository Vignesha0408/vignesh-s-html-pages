Information Retrieval Cheat Sheet

Information Retrieval Components

ComponentDescriptionFunctionExamples
Document CollectionSet of documents to be searchedStorage of information to be retrievedWeb pages, books, articles, emails
QueryUser's information need expressed in keywordsInput that specifies what information is neededSearch terms, natural language questions
IndexData structure for efficient retrievalMaps terms to documents containing themInverted index, forward index
Retrieval ModelMethod for matching queries to documentsDefines how relevance is calculatedBoolean, Vector space, Probabilistic
Evaluation MetricsMeasures of system effectivenessQuantifies retrieval qualityPrecision, recall, F1-score

Text Preprocessing

StepDescriptionPurposeTechniques
TokenizationBreaking text into individual words/tokensConvert text to processable unitsWhitespace, punctuation-based splitting
Stop Word RemovalRemoving common words with little meaningReduce index size, improve relevanceRemove "the", "and", "is", etc.
StemmingReducing words to their root formGroup related terms togetherPorter Stemmer, Snowball Stemmer
LemmatizationReducing words to their dictionary formMore accurate than stemmingMorphological analysis
Case NormalizationConverting text to consistent caseEnsure case-insensitive matchingConvert to lowercase
Spell CorrectionFixing misspelled wordsImprove query and document matchingLevenshtein distance, n-gram matching

Retrieval Models

ModelApproachAdvantagesDisadvantagesBest For
Boolean ModelExact matching using Boolean operatorsSimple, precise, fastNo partial matching, no rankingDatabase queries, exact matching
Vector Space ModelRepresent documents and queries as vectorsPartial matching, ranking possibleHigh dimensionality, no semanticsMost IR systems
Probabilistic ModelRank documents by probability of relevanceTheoretically grounded, good rankingComplex, parameter estimationHigh-quality retrieval
Language ModelsEstimate probability of generating query from documentSimple, no term independence assumptionQuery likelihood, smoothing neededSmaller collections
Learning to RankMachine learning for rankingLeverages multiple featuresRequires training data, complexWeb search engines

Indexing Techniques

TechniqueDescriptionStructureAdvantagesDisadvantages
Inverted IndexMap terms to documents containing themTerm → [doc1, doc2, ...]Fast retrieval, space efficientUpdate overhead
Forward IndexMap documents to terms they containDoc → [term1, term2, ...]Fast indexing, compressionSlow retrieval
Signature FilesBit signatures for documents/termsBit vectors for filteringFast filteringFalse positives
Patricia TriesCompressed prefix treesTree structure for termsFast prefix searchMemory overhead
N-gram IndexingIndex character/word sequencesSubstrings as index termsHandles typos, phrase searchLarge index size

Evaluation Metrics

MetricFormulaDescriptionBest For
PrecisionTP/(TP+FP)Relevant documents among retrieved onesWhen false positives are costly
RecallTP/(TP+FN)Retrieved documents among all relevant onesWhen false negatives are costly
F1-Score2×(Precision×Recall)/(Precision+Recall)Harmonic mean of precision and recallBalanced evaluation
MAP (Mean Average Precision)Average of precision at each relevant docMean precision across multiple queriesSystem evaluation
NDCG (Normalized Discounted Cumulative Gain)Normalized DCG at position kRanking quality with graded relevanceGraded relevance tasks
MRR (Mean Reciprocal Rank)Mean of reciprocal ranks of first relevant docFocus on first relevant resultQuestion answering
