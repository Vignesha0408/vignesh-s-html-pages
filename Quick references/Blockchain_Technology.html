Blockchain Technology - Quick Reference

ConceptDescription
Definition
Distributed ledger technology that maintains records in multiple places at the same time. Records are linked using cryptography and are immutable.
Key Components
Blocks (containing data, timestamp, hash), Chain (linked blocks), Nodes (network participants), Consensus mechanisms
Core Features
Decentralization, Transparency, Immutability, Trustless system, Distributed consensus
Blockchain TypesDescription
Public
Open to anyone (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum). Fully decentralized with no access restrictions.
Private
Restricted access, controlled by single organization. More efficient but less decentralized.
Consortium
Controlled by group of organizations. Balance between public and private blockchains.
Hybrid
Combines elements of public and private blockchains for specific use cases.
Consensus MechanismsDescription
Proof of Work (PoW)
Miners solve cryptographic puzzles to validate transactions (e.g., Bitcoin).
Proof of Stake (PoS)
Validators chosen based on stake/ownership of cryptocurrency.
Delegated PoS
Token holders vote for delegates to validate transactions.
Practical Byzantine Fault Tolerance (pBFT)
Algorithm ensuring system functions even with malicious nodes.
Smart ContractsDescription
Definition
Self-executing contracts with terms directly written into code. Execute automatically when conditions are met.
Platforms
Ethereum, Cardano, Polkadot, Solana, Binance Smart Chain
Use Cases
DeFi, NFTs, Supply chain, Insurance, Real estate, Voting systems
ApplicationsDescription
Cryptocurrency
Digital money using blockchain for secure transactions (Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc.)
Supply Chain
Tracking products from origin to consumer with transparency and authenticity.
Healthcare
Secure patient records, drug traceability, medical data sharing.
Identity Management
Decentralized identity verification and management systems.
ChallengesDescription
Scalability
Transaction speed limitations (TPS) compared to traditional systems.
Energy Consumption
High energy usage in PoW systems (Bitcoin mining).
Regulation
Lack of uniform regulatory framework globally.
Interoperability
Different blockchains struggle to communicate with each other.
Emerging TrendsDescription
Layer 2 Solutions
Scaling solutions like Lightning Network, Polygon for faster transactions.
CBDCs
Central Bank Digital Currencies issued by governments.
Web3
Decentralized internet built on blockchain technology.
NFTs
Non-fungible tokens representing unique digital assets.
